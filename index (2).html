Profile

Bridging Hardware & Intelligent Systems

I'm a PhD candidate at Ontario Tech University's IoT Research Laboratory, supervised by Prof. Khalid Elgazzar. My research centres on interpretable machine learning for edge-constrained ECG monitoring — specifically a white-box Gaussian decomposition framework for Internet of Medical Things (IoMT) applications.

Unlike black-box deep learning models, my work prioritises explainability and sub-millisecond latency on ARM Cortex-M4 hardware, making clinical-grade signal processing viable at the edge — no cloud dependency required.

With 6+ years spanning embedded systems development, AI research, and technical education as a Boot Camp Instructor at Vanderbilt University, I bring both depth and breadth to every engineering challenge.
